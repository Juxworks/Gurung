001 · Our Story

Where the
mountains
meet the cup

Founded with a deep reverence for Himalayan wisdom, Gurung Tea was born from a desire to honour the farming families of Nepal. Our name — a common Nepali surname among the mountain cultivators we partner with — is a daily reminder of who we exist for.

"Should you aspire to do something for a long time, let it be for others and not for yourself."

Each harvest is a collaboration between ancient landscape and living tradition. Our teas are wildcrafted and hand-blended, wrapped in Lokta bark paper made by a cooperative of young Nepali mothers.

004 · The Craft

Wrapped in
Lokta bark,
made with love

Our packaging is as considered as our tea. Each tin is wrapped in traditional Nepali Lokta bark paper — handmade by a community enterprise of young mothers in the Himalayan foothills.

Our pyramid tea bags are knitted from plant-based, compostable, hypoallergenic material. No plastic. No compromise.

  • Hand-harvested at high altitude
  • Sun-dried, never machine-processed
  • Pyramid bags from plant-based material
  • Tins wrapped in hand-pressed Lokta paper
  • Partnered with ANSAB Foundation, Nepal

005 · Our Mission

Tea as a
force for good

Gurung Tea exists at the intersection of craft, conscience, and community. We believe that what we buy should reflect the world we want to live in — one where the artisans are honoured, the land is protected, and pleasure and purpose are inseparable.

In partnership with the ANSAB Foundation, we support poverty alleviation and biodiversity conservation across the Himalayan foothills.

Learn about Gurung Care

01

Community First

Named for the Gurung families who grow our teas. We pay fair wages, build lasting partnerships, and share a portion of every sale back into these communities.

02

Ecological Stewardship

Wild plants thrive in wild places. We protect biodiversity by farming with — never against — the Himalayan ecosystem.

03

Zero Compromise

Every decision — from cultivation to packaging — is held to the same standard: is this the most honourable way we could do this?
